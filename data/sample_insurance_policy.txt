The Star Health Comprehensive Insurance Policy is one of India's most widely purchased individual and family health insurance plans.

Three reasons customers choose Star Health:

- Lifelong renewability — no upper age limit on renewal.

SECTION 4 — WHAT IS COVERED
----------------------------------------------------
In-patient hospitalisation:
- All medical expenses for hospitalisation lasting more than 24 hours.
- Covers room rent, ICU charges, surgeon fees, anaesthesia, nursing charges, diagnostic tests, medicines, and consumables.
- Single private AC room — no sub-limit on room rent.

Pre-hospitalisation expenses:
- Covered for up to 60 days before admission.
- Includes diagnostic tests, doctor consultations, and medicines directly related to the condition being treated.

Post-hospitalisation expenses:
- Covered for up to 90 days after discharge.
- Includes follow-up consultations, medicines, and tests related to the treated condition.

Day care procedures:
- Over 400 day care treatments covered — procedures that do not require 24-hour hospitalisation.
- Examples: cataract surgery, chemotherapy, dialysis, lithotripsy, endoscopy, minor surgeries.

Maternity cover:
- Normal delivery and caesarean section expenses covered.
- Waiting period: 24 months from policy inception.
- Newborn baby cover from birth up to 90 days included.
- Both mother's delivery expenses and newborn's initial medical needs covered.

AYUSH treatment:
- In-patient expenses for Ayurveda, Yoga, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy covered up to the sum insured.
- Treatment must be received at a recognised AYUSH hospital.

Emergency ambulance:
- Ambulance charges for emergency transport to hospital are covered.

Mental illness:
- Hospitalisation for mental illness and psychiatric conditions covered as per Mental Healthcare Act 2017.

Organ donor:
- Medical expenses for the donor's surgery covered when the insured is the transplant recipient.

Second medical opinion:
- Cost of obtaining a second opinion from a specialist is covered.

Modern treatments:
- Robotic surgery, oral chemotherapy, stem cell therapy (specified conditions), immunotherapy, and deep brain stimulation covered.

Restoration of sum insured:
- 100 percent restoration of sum insured once per policy year for a different illness in the same year.
- Ensures the family does not run out of coverage mid-year.

Annual health check-up:
- One free health check-up per adult member per year after two claim-free policy years.

Bariatric surgery:
- Weight-loss surgical procedures and their complications covered.
- Waiting period: 3 years.

SECTION 5 — WHAT IS NOT COVERED (EXCLUSIONS)
----------------------------------------------------
The following are NOT covered:

Pre-existing diseases (during waiting period):
- Any illness or condition that existed before the policy purchase date is not covered during the pre-existing disease waiting period of 3 years.
- After the 3-year waiting period on continuous renewal, pre-existing diseases are fully covered.

Initial 30-day waiting period:
- Any illness contracted within the first 30 days from policy start is not covered.
- Exception: accidents are covered from day one.

Specified disease waiting period (24 months):
- Hernia, cataract, joint replacement, kidney stones, sinusitis, varicose veins, and other listed conditions have a 24-month waiting period.

Cosmetic and aesthetic procedures:
- Liposuction, Botox, implants, facelifts, and similar procedures excluded.
- Exception: cosmetic repair required due to an accident or cancer treatment is covered.

Dental treatment:
- Dental and oral surgery excluded unless caused by accidental injury.

Self-inflicted injury:
- Hospitalisation due to intentional self-harm or suicide attempts not covered.

War, terrorism, nuclear events:
- Injuries from war, terrorism, nuclear weapons, or civil unrest excluded.

Adventure and hazardous sports:
- Injuries from bungee jumping, skydiving, mountaineering, racing excluded.

Vaccination and preventive care:
- Routine vaccinations, inoculations, and preventive check-ups excluded.

Substance abuse:
- Treatment due to alcohol, drugs, or other intoxicating substances excluded.

Infertility and IVF:
- Infertility treatment, IVF, IUI, and assisted reproduction are not covered.

Obesity treatment:
- Non-surgical weight management and obesity-related treatments excluded (bariatric surgery covered after waiting period).

SECTION 6 — WAITING PERIODS (SUMMARY)
----------------------------------------------------
- Initial waiting period: 30 days for any non-accidental illness. Accidents covered from day one.
- Pre-existing disease (PED) waiting period: 3 years (36 months) of continuous coverage. After this, all pre-existing conditions fully covered.
- Specified disease waiting period: 24 months for hernia, cataract, joint replacement, kidney stones, sinusitis, varicose veins, and similar listed conditions.
- Maternity waiting period: 24 months from policy inception.
- Bariatric surgery waiting period: 3 years.

Important: Waiting periods already served do not restart when the policy is continuously renewed without a break. If a customer switches from another insurer, IRDAI portability rules allow credit for waiting periods already completed.

----------------------------------------------------
SECTION 7 — RIDERS AND ADD-ONS
----------------------------------------------------
1. Reduction in PED Waiting Period Rider:
- Reduces the pre-existing disease waiting period from 3 years to 1 year.
- Available at an additional premium, must be selected at policy inception.
- Highly recommended for customers with known health conditions.

2. Personal Accident Cover:
- Lump-sum benefit for accidental death or permanent disability.
- Amount is separate from health sum insured — adds financial protection on top.

3. Critical Illness Rider:
- Lump-sum payout on first diagnosis of listed critical illnesses: cancer, heart attack, stroke, kidney failure, major organ transplant.
- Paid in addition to any hospitalisation claim.

4. Hospital Cash Benefit:
- Fixed daily cash allowance for each day of hospitalisation.
- Helps offset incidental expenses: food, transport, income loss during recovery.

5. Super Star Saver (Top-Up):
- Activates when the base sum insured is exhausted.
- Provides additional cover at a lower incremental premium.

SECTION 9 — CLAIM PROCESS
----------------------------------------------------
Cashless claim at a network hospital:
1. For planned hospitalisation: inform Star Health at least 48 hours in advance via the 24x7 helpline or Star Health mobile app.
2. For emergencies: inform Star Health within 24 hours of admission.
3. Show Star Health insurance card or policy details at the hospital's insurance desk.
4. Hospital raises a pre-authorisation request with Star Health directly.
5. Star Health approves and settles the bill with the hospital at discharge. Customer pays only for non-covered items.
6. 90 percent of cashless claims are settled within 2 hours.

Reimbursement claim (non-network hospital):
1. Keep all original bills, diagnostic reports, prescriptions, discharge summary, and doctor certificates.
2. File the reimbursement claim form within 15 days of discharge.
3. Submit to nearest Star Health branch or upload via the Star Health app or website.
4. Approved amount transferred to the insured's bank account after review.

24x7 helpline available for claims support and general queries.

SECTION 11 — PORTABILITY AND RENEWAL
----------------------------------------------------
Portability:
- Customers switching from any other IRDAI-registered insurer to Star Health can carry over waiting period credits.
- IRDAI mandates that waiting periods already served under a prior policy are honoured.
- Application for portability must be made at least 45 days before the existing policy's renewal date.

Renewal:
- Annual renewal. No upper age limit — lifelong renewability guaranteed.
- Continuous renewal preserves all waiting period credits.
- A break in coverage resets waiting periods.

SECTION 13 — ELIGIBILITY BOUNDARY AND PRODUCT ROUTING
----------------------------------------------------
The Star Health Comprehensive Insurance Policy has a maximum entry age of 65 years.

Customers who are 66 years or older at first purchase are NOT eligible for the Comprehensive plan.
They must instead be offered the Star Health Senior Citizens Red Carpet Health Insurance Plan.

A customer aged 60 to 65 may choose either the Comprehensive plan or the Senior Citizens Red Carpet plan, depending on their health profile and preference. The advisor helps decide.

If the customer's age is not yet confirmed, always ask before recommending a product.

SECTION 14 — STAR HEALTH SENIOR CITIZENS RED CARPET HEALTH INSURANCE PLAN
----------------------------------------------------
This plan is specifically designed for Indian customers aged 60 to 75 years who cannot or do not want the standard Comprehensive plan.

Entry age: 60 to 75 years at first purchase.
Renewability: Lifelong — the policy can be renewed every year with no upper age limit.

Sum insured options:
- 1 lakh, 2 lakh, 3 lakh, 4 lakh, 5 lakh, 7.5 lakh, 10 lakh, 15 lakh, 25 lakh.

What is covered:
- In-patient hospitalisation for illnesses and accidents.
- Single private AC room — no sub-limit on room rent.
- Pre-hospitalisation expenses: up to 30 days before admission.
- Post-hospitalisation expenses: up to 60 days after discharge.
- Day care procedures: over 400 procedures not requiring 24-hour stay (e.g. cataract surgery, dialysis, chemotherapy).
- Emergency ambulance charges covered.
- Annual health check-up: one free check-up per year included.
- AYUSH (Ayurveda, Yoga, Unani, Siddha, Homeopathy): covered at recognised AYUSH hospitals.

Co-payment — MANDATORY and non-negotiable:
- 30 percent co-payment applies on all admissible claims.
- This means: the customer pays 30 percent of every eligible claim; Star Health pays 70 percent.
- Co-payment is a standard IRDAI-permitted feature of senior citizen health insurance in India. It cannot be waived. It is not a defect — it allows senior citizens to get meaningful coverage at a more affordable premium.
- Example: If the claim is Rs. 1 lakh, the customer pays Rs. 30,000 and Star Health pays Rs. 70,000.
- This must always be explained to the customer before they agree to a callback.

Waiting periods for the Senior Citizens Red Carpet plan:
- Initial waiting period: 30 days for any non-accidental illness. Accidents are covered from day one.
- Pre-existing disease (PED) waiting period: 12 months. Named and specified pre-existing conditions (such as diabetes, hypertension, thyroid, asthma — as declared at time of purchase) are covered after 12 months of continuous coverage.
- Specified disease waiting period: 12 months for listed conditions including hernia, cataract, joint replacement, kidney stones, and sinusitis. This is a reduction from the 24-month wait in the standard Comprehensive plan.
- There is no PED waiting period reduction rider available for this plan.

Premium range (indicative only — exact quote from advisor):
- Premium depends on exact age, city, sum insured, and health declaration.
- Indicative range: Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 1,20,000 or more per year.
- For a 65-year-old, 10 lakh sum insured: approximately Rs. 40,000 to Rs. 60,000 per year.
- For a 70-year-old, 10 lakh sum insured: approximately Rs. 60,000 to Rs. 90,000 per year.
- GST at 18 percent is applicable on top of the premium.
- The daily cost equivalent: roughly Rs. 110 to Rs. 250 per day depending on age and cover chosen.

Tax benefit under Section 80D:
- Senior citizens can claim a tax deduction of up to Rs. 50,000 on health insurance premiums paid.
- This is higher than the standard Rs. 25,000 deduction for non-senior adults.
- Confirm exact eligibility with a CA.

Medical tests before issuance:
- Medical tests may be required depending on age and sum insured chosen.
- The advisor confirms the test requirements at proposal stage.

What is NOT covered (same major exclusions as Comprehensive plan):
- Cosmetic and aesthetic procedures.
- Dental treatment (except accidental injury).
- Self-inflicted injuries.
- War, terrorism, nuclear events.
- Adventure and hazardous sports injuries.
- Infertility and IVF.
- Vaccination and preventive care.

Important note on the 30 percent co-payment:
- At claim time, after Star Health approves the claim amount, the customer's share is 30 percent.
- If Star Health does not approve a part of the claim (e.g. non-covered items), that portion is also fully the customer's cost.
- Customers should plan for this co-payment when estimating their out-of-pocket exposure.

SECTION 15 — NO CLAIM BONUS
----------------------------------------------------
Under the Star Health Comprehensive Insurance Policy, a No Claim Bonus (NCB) applies:
- If no claim is made in a policy year, the sum insured increases by 10 percent for the next year at no extra premium, up to a maximum bonus of 50 percent.
- If a claim is made in a year, the accumulated bonus reduces by 10 percent for the next year.
- NCB applies on the base sum insured, not on rider benefits.
- NCB does not apply on the Senior Citizens Red Carpet plan — confirm with advisor.

----------------------------------------------------
SECTION 16 — SECTION 80D TAX DEDUCTION (COMPREHENSIVE PLAN)
----------------------------------------------------
Health insurance premiums paid for the Comprehensive plan are eligible for deduction under Section 80D of the Income Tax Act:
- Self, spouse, and dependent children: deduction up to Rs. 25,000 per year.
- If the insured is a senior citizen (60 years or above): deduction up to Rs. 50,000 per year.
- Premium paid for parents who are senior citizens: additional deduction up to Rs. 50,000 per year.
- Premium paid for parents who are not senior citizens: additional deduction up to Rs. 25,000 per year.
- Maximum total deduction possible: Rs. 1,00,000 per year (self as senior + senior parents).
- This is a tax benefit, not a premium subsidy. Consult a CA for exact applicability.
